# Draft Client Demo Email

Use this prompt skill when the user wants to email a prospect or client about a website audit, demo website makeover, or proposed next steps for Buffaly-powered website/SEO/marketing services.

## Goal

Write a concise, credible email that explains what was reviewed, what was improved or demonstrated, why it matters for the business, and what the next step would be if they want help.

## Inputs To Resolve

- Business name
- Recipient name/contact, if known
- Existing website URL
- Audit summary or key findings
- Demo URL, if available
- Recommended next step
- Desired tone: helpful, direct, consultative, warm, or sales-oriented
- Whether this is cold outreach, warm follow-up, or client delivery

Do not invent personal relationship details, results, ratings, or business claims.

## Email Structure

1. Subject line
2. Short opening that names the business/site
3. One or two specific observations from the audit
4. Explain that a demo or improvement plan was prepared, if applicable
5. Mention the practical business benefit: more trust, clearer services, stronger local visibility, easier quote/contact path, or better lead conversion
6. Link to the demo or offer to share/review it
7. Simple next-step CTA
8. Friendly close

## Cold Outreach Guidance

For a first cold email:

- Keep it short.
- Avoid sounding like a generic SEO blast.
- Mention one specific finding.
- Say what was created in plain language.
- Ask for a small next step, such as permission to send the demo or schedule a 10-minute review.

## Warm Follow-Up Guidance

For a warm or existing-client email:

- Summarize completed review/demo work.
- Explain what the next implementation phase would include.
- Offer clear options: quick fixes, full refresh, local SEO cleanup, or ongoing support.

## Optional Variants

When useful, include:

- Short follow-up email
- SMS version
- LinkedIn message version
- More consultative version
- More direct sales version

## Style

- Human, specific, and useful.
- No hype.
- No scare tactics.
- Avoid jargon like schema, NAP, metadata, or technical SEO unless the recipient is technical.
- Frame Buffaly as a practical assistant/service platform that helps make high-quality marketing affordable for small businesses.

## Default CTA Examples

- Would you like me to send over the demo?
- Want me to walk you through the audit and demo this week?
- If useful, we can turn this into a simple implementation plan and start with the highest-impact fixes.
- Would a 10-minute review be helpful?
